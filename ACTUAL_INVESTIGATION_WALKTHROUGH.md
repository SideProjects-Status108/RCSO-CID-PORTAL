# Actual Investigation Walkthrough - Detective's Real Experience

## Ground Truth from Stanley's Perspective

**Date:** Saturday, July 12, 2025  
**Context:** Stanley is DIT (Detective In Training) with FTO Quintal at RCSO CID (started phase training 2025). Previously Metro Nashville detective for several years. Quintal is FTO but Stanley has more LE experience.

---

## T+0 (~9:31am) — RADIO DISPATCH & INITIAL TEXT

### What Happened:

Stanley and partner (Quintal) are leaving a natural death scene. Radio call:

- "Wreck with injuries on I-24 westbound near Rutherford/Davidson county line"
- "Multiple callers"
- Responders: Metro Police, THP, LaVergne City Police
- "County also getting dispatched"
- Update: "Possible shooting"

### Text Message Received (9:31am):

Sgt. Amanda McPherson texts group (3 people: Stanley, Quintal, Crime Scene Tech Kendall):

```
"Can yall head to the interstate for this shooting? The ADA has now requested we go. 
Kendall can you get the truck"
```

Responses in thread show coordination. Stanley is NOT designated lead but is part of responding team.

### Key Problem #1: Information Vacuum

Stanley knows:

- It's a shooting on I-24
- ADA requested response
- That's literally all

Stanley does NOT know:

- Exact location (just "I-24 west, Rutherford/Davidson line")
- Victim info
- Suspect info
- Whether this is Tier 1, 2, or 3
- Who is lead detective
- What he's supposed to do when he arrives
- That Metro already has detectives at scene

---

## T+0:30 (~10:00am) — DRIVE TO SCENE: THE CHAOS MULTIPLIES

### What Stanley Actually Did:

Drove from Murfreesboro (I-24 exit 80) toward county line (north). Heavy Saturday traffic on I-24.

While driving in emergency traffic, Stanley tries to TEXT other detectives:

- Warning: "Traffic is extremely heavy, come down Murfreesboro Rd to Hobson"
- Running emergency lights, emergency mode
- Trying to text while driving
- **Siren breaking up speech-to-text**
- **Dangerous situation:** Breaking phone laws, but in performance of duty

### Key Problem #2: Communication Infrastructure Breaks Down

- Can't use speech-to-text (siren interference)
- Texting while driving emergency (illegal but trying to help)
- Other detectives don't get routing/traffic info
- How many other RCSO detectives are heading there? Stanley doesn't know.

---

## T+1:00 (11:33am) — ARRIVAL AT SCENE: MULTI-AGENCY CHAOS

### What Stanley Saw:

Arriving at scene on I-24, shoulders of road:

- **Multiple LE agencies already present:** LaVergne, Metro, THP, Fire, Ambulance
- **Two civilian vehicles** parked on shoulder
- **All westbound traffic completely cleared** (eerie empty highway)
- **Scene already active** with first responders and detectives working

### Critical Discovery:

THP Sgt. tells Stanley: "The victim and his truck are in the ravine about 30 yards into the ditch into the woods, the rest of them are all down there."

Stanley asks: "Who's all down there?"

THP Sgt: "Uh, Lavergne and Metro, pretty much everybody."

### Key Problem #3: Jurisdiction Chaos at the Scene

- Multiple agencies already documenting scene
- Unclear who's in charge
- Metro detectives (including Det. Liedtke) already doing investigative work
- No coordination between agencies
- LaVergne Police there (which shouldn't be, per policy)

---

## T+1:10 (11:43am) — THE AUTHORITY MOMENT

### Stanley's Decision:

Stanley tells THP Sgt: "Unless they are County Detectives, don't let anyone else down there."

### Immediate Phone Call from LT:

LT calls Stanley (urgently):

```
"Stanley, we have been given full investigatory authority. 
Get all those motherfuckers out of our scene. 
Authority of me and by authority of District Attorney Jones."
```

**Then call drops mid-sentence.**

### Key Problem #4: Stanley's Position

- He's a DIT (detective in training), technically junior
- He has NO information about what happened
- He has NO team present yet (Quintal and others still in traffic)
- He's now responsible for taking charge of a multi-agency scene
- He has to remove other law enforcement from the scene
- He has to do this while confused about the situation himself

---

## T+1:20 (11:53am) — TAKING COMMAND OF CHAOS

### What Stanley Did:

Stanley walks down into ravine/crime scene, approaches the body (on gurney, surrounded by first responders).

Shouts authoritatively (in his words, "the most authoritative voice I could muster at 0735 on a Saturday morning"):

```
"Everyone! Everyone! I am Detective Stanley! 
By Authority of the District Attorney of the 16th District, 
Rutherford County is in charge of this crime scene. 
I need everyone to kindly exit this crime scene, 
but do not leave until your name, badge number and agency 
has been recorded."
```

### Reactions:

- Looks from responders: "Who the hell are you?"
- But Stanley holds ground

### Finding the Current Lead:

Voice from crowd: "I am sir, Detective Liedtke, LPD"

**Stanley now has to:**

1. Take over a scene from Metro Police detective
2. Get briefed quickly
3. Get Liedtke's notes/documentation
4. Extract all non-county personnel
5. Do all this while managing constant phone interruptions

---

## T+1:30-1:45 (12:00-12:15pm) — PHONE INTERRUPTION CHAOS

### First Call: LT (Urgent Update)

Stanley calls back LT with status:

```
Stanley: "Sir, the body has already been removed from the vehicle."
LT: "What?!?" [audible anger and frustration]
Stanley: "Yes sir, I know. I wanted you to know as soon as possible."
LT: "Stanley, do the best you can and unfuck this situation."
Stanley: "Yes sir, direct."
```

### Key Problem #5: CRITICAL CHAIN OF CUSTODY ISSUE

- Body was moved BEFORE county detectives arrived
- Who moved it? When? Why? Unknown
- Photos of victim IN vehicle: LOST
- Victim position documentation: INCOMPLETE
- This information cannot be recovered

### Second Call: Sgt. Amanda

Phone rings again while Stanley is getting briefed from Liedtke.

Stanley gives Liedtke "the look we all know" (get interrupted again).

```
Stanley: "Ma'am I just got off the phone with Lt. 
I am meeting with the current lead exchanging info and trying to get caught up."
Sgt Amanda: "Ok, see you in a bit"
Stanley: "Yes, ma'am"
```

### Key Problem #6: Information Bottleneck

- Stanley is the ONLY RCSO detective at scene
- Multiple agency personnel need to be coordinated out
- Stanley needs to get briefed on situation
- LT and Sgt. both trying to reach him for status
- He's getting information from handwritten notes from Liedtke

---

## T+2:00 (12:30pm) — THE HANDOFF

### What Stanley Received:

Det. Liedtke hands over his **handwritten notes** (chicken scratch, hard to read).

These notes contain:

- Initial observations
- Victim info
- Vehicle info
- Witness info
- Any preliminary investigation

### What Happens Next:

Eventually Sgt. McPherson arrives at scene. Stanley briefs her.

Sgt. McPherson then **manually transcribes** Liedtke's handwritten notes onto **the whiteboard in the war room**.

### Key Problem #7: Information Loss & Manual Transcription

- Handwritten notes are unstructured
- Chicken scratch hard to read
- Transcription is slow
- Information may be lost or misread
- Whiteboard becomes source of truth (but it's a hand-copied version of handwritten notes)
- What if Sgt. misreads something?

---

## T+2:30+ (1:00pm+) — WAITING FOR TEAM

Stanley notes: **"It felt like a lifetime before my team started showing up..."**

Stanley is alone, managing:

- Multi-agency scene evacuation
- Evidence chain of custody (already broken)
- LT and Sgt. phone calls
- Getting briefed on an incident he knows nothing about
- No task list
- No evidence tracker
- No team support

---

### Jurisdiction/Authority Issues:

- Stanley (DIT) has to take command from Metro detective
- Multi-agency coordination happening ad-hoc at scene

### Information Management:

- Stanley doesn't know what happened (no briefing before arrival)
- LT giving orders by phone while scene is active
- Sgt. calling for status while Stanley is getting oriented
- Notes are handwritten, unstructured, hard to transcribe
- No evidence tracker started
- No timeline started
- No task list

### Evidence/Chain of Custody:

- Body already removed from vehicle (critical loss)
- Who moved it? When? Why? Unknown/not documented
- Vehicle left unattended during scene transfer
- Initial documentation missing
- Evidence collection not yet organized

### Team Coordination:

- Stanley is alone for extended period
- Other RCSO detectives stuck in traffic
- No routing info provided
- No team assignments
- No task distribution

### Communication:

- Phone interruptions while Stanley working scene
- Speech-to-text not working (siren interference)
- Text messages as primary communication
- No structured incident coordination
